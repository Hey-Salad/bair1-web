# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Health-conscious individuals and families in urban areas (London-first) who want to understand the air they breathe. They range from parents worried about pollution near schools to runners checking conditions before a jog. Not scientists — real people who want a clear, honest answer: "Is the air good right now?"

## Product Purpose

Bair1 is a consumer air quality platform: a physical sensor (teddy bear form factor) paired with a web dashboard. The sensor plugs in, connects to WiFi, and publishes PM1/PM2.5/PM10 readings to bair1.live. The bear's expression and orb colour change with the air — one glance tells you everything. Success looks like: a person checks Bair1 before opening their window, and trusts the answer.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, honest, grounded.

Like a trusted neighbour who happens to have a particulate matter sensor. The bear mascot is warm but not childish — it's a character with a job (sniffing the air), not a decoration. The brand earns trust through transparency: real readings, clear guidance, no jargon.

Voice: conversational, direct, occasionally dry. Never corporate, never cutesy. "The air's not great today" over "Air quality index has entered the moderate threshold."

## Anti-references

- Generic IoT / smart home: No glossy renders, no "smart living" stock photography, no rounded-everything Apple-clone aesthetic. Bair1 is a specific object with a specific job, not a lifestyle accessory.
- Government / institutional: No clinical blue-and-white, no dense data tables as the primary interface, no bureaucratic feel. This is for people, not compliance officers.
- Kiddie / cartoon: Despite the bear, this is not a children's toy. The bear is a mascot with personality, not a plush. Avoid infantilizing the design.
- SaaS dashboard startup: No purple gradients, no "trusted by 10,000 teams", no generic B2B aesthetic. This is a consumer product for individuals and families.

## Design Principles

1. **One glance, full picture.** Every screen should communicate its core message in under 2 seconds. The bear's expression, the orb colour, the guidance text — all redundant signals pointing at the same truth.
2. **Honest by default.** Show real data, real readings, real guidance. No vanity metrics, no gamification, no "streaks." If the air is bad, say so plainly.
3. **Grounded in the physical.** The brand bridges hardware and software. Design should feel tactile and specific — like the sensor itself — not floaty or abstract.
4. **Respect the viewer.** No dark patterns, no notification spam, no data harvesting theatre. Accessibility is baseline, not a feature.
5. **Distinctive, not decorative.** Every design choice should serve the brand's identity. If it could belong to any other air quality product, it's not specific enough.

## References

- Teenage Engineering: Industrial design meets playfulness. Specific product photography, bold typography, unapologetic aesthetic choices. The "tool as personality" energy.
- Patagonia: Earthy, activist, honest. Nature-first brand that's serious but warm. Environmental mission without preachiness.
- PurpleAir: The air quality space incumbent. Bair1 should feel like the version of PurpleAir that hired a real designer.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- All AQI states must be distinguishable without colour alone (shape, text, position)
- Reduced motion alternatives for all animations
- Screen reader support for all data visualisations
